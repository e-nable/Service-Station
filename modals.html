ABOUT HAND-O-MATIC

Hand-o-matic allows you to:

  • Enter your measurements
  • Select different models of hand, such as for people with no thumb
  • Preview the parts
  • Generate 3D printable STL files that get emailed to the email address provided

Hand-o-matic does NOT:

  • Generate a request to order manufacturing of this product
  • Talk to a 3D printer

If you would like an e-NABLE volunteer to work with you through this process, or if your needs are not addressed by Hand-o-matic, please fill out the e-NABLE Intake Form.

Help with Measurements

Measure your knuckle width (R8) and palm length (R9) as shown by the labeled arrows in the photograph.

Measurements should be in millimeters (mm). To convert inches to millimeters, multiply by 25.4. For example, 3.5 inches is 88.9 mm.

Please reference the current generic measurement guide for more details on measurements.

If you would like an e-NABLE volunteer to work with you through this process, or if your needs are not addressed by Hand-o-matic, please fill out the e-NABLE Intake Form.

Help with Model Selection

First select the “palm style” that suits your needs. The palm is the part that covers your hand. The default is “Raptor Hand”. For more information about the different designs and options that are available, see e-NABLE Upper Limb Prosthetics.

If you have a thumb, then you should select a prosthetic design labeled “no thumb”.

Some designs have “support” and “no support” versions. You should typically select the “support” version. The “no support” version is provided for those that prefer to generate their own support structures in their printer’s software.

Second, select a matching finger style.

Third, select a gauntlet style. The gauntlet is the part that covers your arm.

Then enter the thickness of the padding that you will be using. 5mm is a typical thickness.

You can view the complete prosthetic, or individual parts by selecting them from the “What would you like to view” menu.

When you have a selection that you are happy with, enter your email address and click “Send” to have a link to the files emailed to you.
